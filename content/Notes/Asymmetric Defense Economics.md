Small governance entities can achieve effective defense capabilities through strategic use of low-cost, expendable systems rather than expensive military platforms.

- Modern defense capabilities no longer require massive capital investment in traditional military equipment.
- The success of Ukraine's use of commercial drones demonstrates how consumer technology can be repurposed for defense.
- The economic exchange ratio between low-cost systems and expensive countermeasures favors the smaller actor.
- This shift enables new forms of governance to maintain security without traditional military-industrial infrastructure.
