Small communities united by specific interests can provide more value than large cities with diffuse connections.

- Concentrated networks of like-minded people can create stronger bonds than dispersed urban populations.
- Shared interests enable rapid iteration and development of both technical and social technologies.
- The quality of connections matters more than the quantity when building effective communities.
