Critical infrastructure controlled through remote software presents a novel vector for exercising power over sovereign entities.

- Physical infrastructure can be compromised through software control systems even when locally owned.
- Remote control of critical systems like port cranes creates leverage without requiring local presence.
- Modern infrastructure's connectivity creates new vulnerabilities that traditional sovereignty models don't address.
- The ability to remotely operate or disable critical infrastructure represents a new form of practical governance authority.
