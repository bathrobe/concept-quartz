Control over digital infrastructure represents a new form of geopolitical power that transcends traditional military strength.
- Nations can achieve strategic dominance by providing and controlling fundamental digital systems like telecommunications, cloud services, and enterprise software.
- The ability to offer "free" digital infrastructure creates dependencies that can reshape entire societies more effectively than military force.
- Digital infrastructure control enables influence at multiple levels: structural, digital, and financial.
