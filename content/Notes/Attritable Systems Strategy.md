title: Attritable Systems Strategy
sourceQuote: The Iranian Shahedron costs $180,000. So every time we shoot eight million dollars of Patriot missiles at a $180,000 drone, we are losing whether or not you destroy that asset.
sourceAttribution: "[[Trae Stephens]]"
sourceLink: "[[Sources/Rebuilding the Arsenal of Democracy - Reboot Conference]]"
---

Expendable, low-cost systems can create asymmetric advantages by forcing opponents to expend disproportionate resources in response.

- The cost ratio between attack and defense systems becomes a strategic consideration itself.
- Mass-produced simple systems can overwhelm more sophisticated defenses through sheer numbers.
- The psychological impact of losing systems becomes negligible when they're designed to be expendable.
- This approach shifts resource allocation from maintaining expensive platforms to maintaining production capacity.
