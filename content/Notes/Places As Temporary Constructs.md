Physical spaces can function as temporary platforms for community rather than permanent destinations.

- Modern communities can materialize and dematerialize in different locations while maintaining their identity.
- The concept of "place" becomes more about the intentional gathering of like-minded people than geographic coordinates.
- Digital-first communities can treat physical locations as temporary stages for deeper connection rather than permanent homes.
- Success of a place comes from its ability to serve a specific community's needs at a specific time, not from historical legacy.
