#### Summary
A panel of national security experts discussed global power dynamics, focusing on China's rising influence and America's preparedness for potential conflicts, particularly regarding Taiwan.

#### Main points
- The panelists emphasized China as the primary global competitor to the US, with concerns about its technological and economic influence.
- Multiple speakers highlighted the urgent need to prepare for a potential Taiwan conflict scenario within the next year rather than decades.
- The discussion emphasized America's need for stronger deterrence and industrial preparedness, particularly in critical minerals and manufacturing.
- Panelists expressed concern about current US leadership's reluctance to clearly identify adversaries and articulate strategic objectives.

#### Bullet summary
- The panel discussed the shift from a unipolar to multipolar world order, with China as the main rising power.
- Iran was identified as a country that receives outsized attention relative to its actual strategic importance.
- Panelists highlighted the need for dramatically increased defense spending and nuclear modernization.
- The discussion touched on non-traditional threats like fentanyl trafficking from China through Mexico.
- Multiple speakers expressed concern about foreign sabotage teams potentially operating within US borders.
- Critical mineral supply chains and manufacturing capabilities were identified as urgent priorities.
- The panel discussed the need for market protections through tariffs and domestic manufacturing incentives.
- Speakers debated the impact of regulatory constraints on American technological competitiveness.
- There was significant focus on Taiwan as a potential near-term conflict scenario.
- The discussion concluded with debate about American leadership and strategic vision.
