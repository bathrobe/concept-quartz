#### Summary
Vitalik Buterin describes Zuzalu, an experimental two-month pop-up community in Montenegro that explored how online tribes can materialize into physical spaces and create new forms of intentional communities.

#### Main points
- Digital communities can create their own physical spaces rather than being constrained by existing geographical locations.
- Small focused communities can provide better network effects than large unfocused cities for specific interests.
- The longer duration of Zuzalu (2 months vs typical week-long conferences) created a fundamentally different social dynamic.
- The experiment succeeded in creating international diversity while demonstrating the viability of intentional communities.

#### Bullet summary
- Zuzalu housed 200 people for two months in a Montenegro resort as an experiment in community building.
- The community focused on three main groups: Ethereum developers, biotech/longevity researchers, and European rationalists.
- The project used novel technologies like Zupass for identity management using zero-knowledge proofs.
- Community members self-organized many activities including cold plunges and cooking groups.
- The event demonstrated that dedicated enthusiasts can effectively beta-test both technical and social technologies.
- While successful as a gathering, Zuzalu didn't fully realize goals like crypto payments or DAO governance.
- No single country made up more than one-third of attendees, with the US and China being the top two sources.
- The experiment showed promise but raised questions about scaling beyond niche communities.
- Participants were primarily young, with few families, and included many digital nomads.
- The author suggests Zuzalu might evolve to combine aspects of universities, monasteries, and technology incubators.

#### People, Places, and Things
**Zuzalu** - A two-month experimental community in Montenegro combining technology, longevity research, and community building.

**Zupass** - An identity system using zero-knowledge proofs developed for Zuzalu residents.

**Michel Maffesoli** - French sociologist who wrote about the rise of interest-based tribes.

**Balaji Srinivasan** - Author of "The Network State" about online communities materializing into physical spaces.

**Bryan Johnson** - Longevity researcher whose "Blueprint menu" influenced Zuzalu's food program.

**Montenegro** - Southeastern European country where Zuzalu was located.

#### Interesting Details
- The resort was made affordable by negotiating bulk rates and choosing off-season timing.
- Daily meals were limited to $15 per person while trying to follow longevity principles.
- One apartment's balcony was converted into a community gym.
- Less than 1% of Russians left their country even during wartime, showing how rare large-scale voluntary migration is.
