1. Core Concept and Background
   - Traditional places and cultures seen as immutable
   - Possibility of intentional culture creation by online tribes
   - Crypto community as example of decentralized geography
   - Need for practical experiment beyond theory

2. Zuzalu Implementation
   - 200 people for 2 months in Montenegro
   - Sweet spot between hacker house and conference
   - Mix of planned structure and organic growth
   - Focus on health, tech, and community building

3. Key Learnings
   - Format success: extended duration created different mindset
   - Technology testing: rapid improvement through real usage
   - International diversity: no country >1/3 of attendees
   - Social technology development through community

4. Challenges and Limitations
   - Demographic biases: young, few families
   - Digital nomad heavy participation
   - Limited crypto payment integration
   - No DAO governance implementation

5. Future Directions
   - Hybrid model combining university, monastery, nomad hub
   - Focus on technology incubation including social tech
   - Need to balance ambition with realistic growth
   - Potential for broader social impact while remaining niche
